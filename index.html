Political Party Favorability

by: Mauhe Almeida

A national survey conducted July 2015 shows that the Republican Party’s image has grown more negative over the first half of the year. Meanwhile, the Democrats continue to hold an advantage over Republicans when it comes to public perception. Democrats are seen as more empathetic and honest while the Republicans are seen as more extreme and radical on their positions. However, neither party has an advantage on public perceptions of who would better manage the federal government, with both having the identical favorability of 40%.

Independents on the Rise

While democrats still have an advantage over republicans in terms of favorability, both parties have lost ground among the public. According to a 2014 survey by Pew Research Center, 39% of Americans now label themselves as independents, outnumbering either Democrats or Republicans.
